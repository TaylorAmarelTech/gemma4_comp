RE: Revocation of employment agency licence of Manco Employment Agency (“Manco”)

We write to seek the immediate revocation of the employment agency licence of Manco (held by licensee WONG Hing-fai Brian) on the basis that Manco has been convicted of overcharging a job seeker as of 4 August 2020.[1] Pursuant to section 53 of the Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57) (“EO”), the Commissioner for Labour (“Commissioner”) may revoke a licence on numerous grounds, including but not limited to, the failure of an employment agency to comply with the Code of Practice for employment agencies. In the present circumstances, the conviction of Manco suffices for the Commissioner to exercise the above mentioned power under the EO. Accordingly, we request that immediate action be taken to prevent Manco from applying for a renewal of its agency licence (or that any application for renewal by Manco be refused).


In view of the judicial determination that Manco has engaged in dishonest operations to the end of exploiting job seekers, we seek a response within 10 days stating, at the least, the steps which your offices will take in this respect.
