Dear Mr. MA Kwong Cho , Ms. IP Ching Man, Ms. TSUI Lai Ching, Kitty, and Ms. CHUNG Lai Ling, Ada:
We write in relation to suspected unlawful activity being committed by Affinity Finance Limited (“Affinity”) and
HALO World Limited (“Halo”). Specifically, after engaging in consumer transactions with Affinity and Halo we have
reason to believe that Halo and Affinity are violating the following ordinances, and licensing conditions.
● Trade Descriptions Ordinance
● Money Lenders Ordinance
● Licensing Conditions for Money Lenders
● Companies Ordinance
In summary, the concerns are outlined for your consideration and it is requested that appointments for statement taking
be scheduled on our days off with an Tagalog interpreter present.
Trade Descriptions Ordinance
1. Halo uses numerous advertisements online and offline. These advertisements showcase mobile phones at
specific prices. However, the advertisements leave out significant material details about the requirements to
purchase these mobile phones at the displayed prices.
Specifically, although Halo’s advertisements state that mobile phones can be purchased at specific prices this
is not exactly true. Upon entering a Halo store, Halo staff will offer to sell customers various mobile phones,
but after a customer agrees to purchase the mobile phone Halo store will inform that customer that they must
also obtain a loan from Affinity in order to receive the mobile phone for purchase. Halo has made obtaining a
loan a requirement even if the customer has enough cash to pay for the full displayed prices.
As a result, customers are falsely led to believe they can purchase mobile phones in cash but then forced to
obtain a loan as well in a deceptive bait and switch scheme. No where in the advertisements of Halo is it
indicated that obtaining a loan from Affinity is a requirement to purchase a phone. Because of this bait and
switch scheme customers are left paying significantly higher prices due to the likely exorbitant interest rates
on the loan that Halo requires customers to obtain. This results in the effective price being significantly higher
than the displayed prices. In addition to the interest, Halo and Affinity charge late fees of HK$20 per day,
these late fees are never disclosed to customers until after a transaction is completed;
Money Lenders Ordinance
1. By charging daily late fees of HK$20 per day, Affinity is effectively charging excessive interest (interest
higher than 60% per annum) in violation of Section 24 of the Money Lenders Ordinance as interpreted in
SKYLINE CREDIT LTD v LEUNG HING CHUNG - [2019] HKCU 311;
2. Despite repeated requests, Affinity generally refuses to provide a copy of the loan agreement in violation of
Section 19 of the Money Lenders Ordinance;
Licensing Conditions for Money Lenders
1. Affinity fails to obtain or request details regarding the third party agreement that the borrower has with Halo
in violation of the 1st licensing condition;
2. Affinity routinely violates the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance by disclosing personal data to third parties
without permission. For example, Affinity has been documented to post the names of all customers on the
public facing side of its door or office premises so anyone from the public could see the full names and details
of customers. Affinity has also disclosed personal information of customers to the customers’ employers
without consent;
3. Affinity fails to obtain written authorization from references before collecting, processing, and using their
personal information in violation of the 13th licensing condition;
Companies Ordinance
1. Affinity and Halo routinely fail to include their full company name and liability status on communication
documents;
2. The full company name and liability status of Halo is not clearly visible on Halo’s website:
http://www.haloworld.hk;
