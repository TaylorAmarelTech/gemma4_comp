Suspected Trafficking In Persons By Talent (Kenya) Company Limited


Background: The Hong Kong registered company Talent (Kenya) Company Limited, its directors, its employees, and relaties parties have engaged in an illegal employment agency scheme whereby:


* FDWs are subject to 4-6 months salary deduction;
* FDWs are instructed by the agency and employers that they are not allowed to have a weekly rest day;
* FDWs have their personal data unlawfully disclosed on social media pages and websites of Talent (Kenya) Company Limited;


Talent (Kenya) Company Limited and its directors subject FDWs to these unlawful deployment and working conditions by actively:


* Withholding passports, contracts, and other personal documents;
* Opening a Bank of China account for FDWs and then retaining the ATM card and authentication credentials;
* Instructing employers to not allow FDWs the ability to use cell phones or take mandatory holidays and rest days;
* Forging the signatures of the FDWs for the purposes of processing payments and submitting employment contracts to the Immigration Department;
* Engaging in deceptive bait and switch marketing techniques and similar unlawful trade descriptions;
* Making threats and harassing FDWs who seek assistance from NGOs or relevant law; enforcement authority, likely constituting criminal intimidation or black mail.


Numbers: It is estimated that the unlawful actions of Talent (Kenya) Company Limited has resulted in:


* At least 200 Kenyan FDWs being brought to Hong Kong as a result of deception;
* At least 200 Kenyan FDWs being subject to illegal agency fees of roughly 24,000 HKD;
* Over 4,800,000 HKD being laundered through suspicious transactions and account activity at Bank of China and Western Union;


No Conclusive Law Enforcement Action: Despite this case being reported to the Employment Agencies Administration (EAA), Hong Kong Police Force (HKPF), and Immigration (ImmD) in September 2018, no concrete action has been taken to stop the agency from this illicit behavior or enforce the relevant Ordinances. Since September 2018, at least 8 Kenyan nationals have reported this case to the EAA, but the EAA has yet to conduct a thorough investigation, initiate prosecution, or even issue a warning letter - as a result the agency continues to operate unlawfully and with almost complete impunity.
