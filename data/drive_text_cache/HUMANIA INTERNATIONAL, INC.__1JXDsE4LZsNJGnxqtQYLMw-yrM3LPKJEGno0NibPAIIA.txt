For: Violation of Republic Act No. 8042, as amended, 2016 POEA Rules and Regulations, Etc.
	REQUEST FOR WRITTEN INTERROGATORIES IN ACCORDANCE WITH DEPARTMENT ORDER NO. 87-08, SERIES OF 2008 ISSUED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF LABOR AND EMPLOYMENT AND OTHER MATTERS


        Complainants submit this filing for consideration by this Forum in relation to the above mentioned case. 


1. The Complainants write to request that this Forum provides them with a mechanism to submit written testimony and evidence in relation to their complaints, in advance of their next scheduled Philippines Overseas Employment Administration (“POEA”) hearing on 29 June 2021. The Philippines Overseas Labour Office in Hong Kong (“POLO-HK”) has notified Complaints that the POEA has not provided written interrogatories to allow the Complainants to submit testimony in writing in accordance with Department Order No. 87-08, Series of 2008 issued by the Department of Labor and Employment, despite numerous requests. Complainants make this joint request to the POEA to expedite the processing and delivery of written interrogatories, given that the hearing is imminent and Complainants should be awarded sufficient time to prepare their written testimony and evidence for submission. 


2. Alternatively, it is requested that the POEA cease from scheduling such hearings, considering that the Complainants are Overseas Filipino Workers (“OFWs”), currently residing in Hong Kong and therefore clearly unable to attend hearings in the Philippines. It is noted that inefficient communication and coordination of POLO-HK and the POEA is in effect hindering OFWs from submitting testimony in writing and evidence in relation to their complaints, as is their right under law. Further, the POEA is clearly not on track to adjudicate the backlog of cases within the mandatory eight month period.


3. Complainants request that the POEA provide referral letters to the National Labor Relation Commission (“NLRC”). Complainants make this request for referral to the NLRC in order to pursue civil claim(s) against the Respondent. In accordance with - G.R. No. 200811 - JULITA M. ALDOVINO, JOAN B. LAGRIMAS, WINNIE B. LINGAT, CHITA A. SALES, SHERLY L. GUINTO, REVILLA S. DE JESUS, AND LAILA V. ORPILLA, PETITIONERS, v. GOLD AND GREEN MANPOWER MANAGEMENT AND DEVELOPMENT SERVICES, INC., SAGE INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT COMPANY, LTD., AND ALBERTO C. ALVINA, RESPONDENTS (“G.R. No. 200811”) - Complainants have the irrevocable right to pursue civil claims in order to recover the full amount owed to them. G.R. No. 200811 specifically states: “Quitclaims do not bar employees from filing labor complaints and demanding benefits to which they are legally entitled. They are "ineffective in barring recovery of the full measure of a worker's rights, and the acceptance of benefits therefrom does not amount to estoppel." The law does not recognize agreements that result in compensation less than what is mandated by law. These quitclaims do not prevent employees from subsequently claiming benefits to which they are legally entitled.” Therefore, Claimants have the inviolable right to pursue a case at the NLRC, regardless of any alleged settlement or quitclaim. In recognition of this, Complainants requests the POEA issue a referral letter to the NLRC.
