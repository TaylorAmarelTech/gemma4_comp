Part 4: Government Maladministration


The following cases are short summaries of money laundering schemes that relate to and/or derive from government maladministration:


(i) POLO and PCG refusing to intake criminal complaints
(ii) SEC refusing to conduct criminal investigations unless complainants attend a conciliation-mediation process
(iii) Refusal of government bodies to intake evidence and complaints without the victims providing testimony in person.
(iv) Nonsensical favoritism


The Philippines has failed to provide an appropriate complaint procedure for overseas Filipino workers in Hong Kong SAR, Taiwan, Singapore, and the Middle East. As a result, the illegal money lenders, illegal agencies, and human trafficking syndicates are not detered because they know when an overseas Filipino worker is outside of the Philippines, neither Philippines Overseas Labor Offices or Philippines Consulates/Embassys are competent/capable of assisting a overseas Filipino worker file a criminal case against the traffickers who reside and store their crime proceeds in the Philippines. For example, POLO-HK has wrongfully discouraged individuals from filing complaints, has inappropriately taken jurisdiction over criminal complaints (when POLO-HK only has jurisdiction over administrative complaints), and has buried complainants in red tape, nonsensical complaint policies and zero transparency. Similarly, the Manila Economic and Cultural Office has failed to take action against illegal lenders, debt bondage syndicates, and modern day slavery/human trafficking schemes whereby agencies in Taiwan and Singapore collude with money lenders to charge exorbitant interest rates for unlawful placement fees.
