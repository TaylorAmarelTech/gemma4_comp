	REQUEST FOR WRITTEN INTERROGATORIES IN ACCORDANCE WITH DEPARTMENT ORDER NO. 87-08, SERIES OF 2008 ISSUED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF LABOR AND EMPLOYMENT AND OTHER MATTERS


        Complainant submits this filing for consideration by this Forum in relation to the above mentioned case. 


1. The Complainant writes to request that this Forum provides them with a mechanism to submit written testimony and evidence in relation to their complaints, in advance of their next scheduled Philippines Overseas Employment Administration (“POEA”) hearings on 24 June & 1 July 2021. The Philippines Overseas Labour Office in Hong Kong (“POLO-HK”) has notified Complaints that the POEA has not provided written interrogatories to allow the Complainant to submit testimony in writing in accordance with Department Order No. 87-08, Series of 2008 issued by the Department of Labor and Employment, despite numerous requests. Complainant makes request to the POEA to expedite the processing and delivery of written interrogatories, given that the hearing is imminent and Complainant should be awarded sufficient time to prepare their written testimony and evidence for submission. 


2. Furthermore, it is requested that the POEA exercise appropriate care when scheduling hearings for Overseas Filipino Workers (“OFWs”) that are currently residing in Hong Kong and therefore clearly unable to attend hearings in the Philippines. The POEA should confirm with OFWs whether or not they or a representative can attend the hearings - absent such confirmation, the POEA should proceed with expeditiously providing written interrogatories to OFWs in overseas jurisdictions. It is noted that inefficient communication and coordination of POLO-HK and the POEA is in effect hindering OFWs from submitting testimony in writing and evidence in relation to their complaints, as is their right under law. Further, the POEA is clearly not on track to adjudicate the backlog of cases within the mandatory eight month period. Moreover, even if the event that Complainant was able to find a representative that could travel to Manila during a COVID-19 pandemic, both POLO-HK and PCG-HK have refused to give Complainant access to pro-bono notarization services or assistance through the Assistance to Nationals Legal Assistance Fund, despite there being a legal and public policy requirement for assistance to be provided to OFWs filing agency complaints.[1]


3. Complainant reiterate the prayer that the Respondent provide a full refund of all fees directly and indirectly charged to Complainant as well as a refund of all fees that Complainant was coerced into paying as a result of Respondent’s unlawful exclusive arrangements with others, such as medical clinics, money lender (where applicable), and training centers. In the event that a civil settlement is reached, Complainant affirm that they wish to continue being a witness in this administrative case and any other criminal case in order to ensure the POEA and other law enforcement agencies can receive appropriate evidence and testimony to uphold the law and hold agencies accountable for their violations of administrative or criminal laws and regulations.
