Concern Regarding Excessive Collection and Online Disclosure of Personal Data by Employment Agencies Placing Foreign Domestic Helpers


To Whom This May Concern,

We have observed extreme and unnecessary disclosures of personal information by HelperDB Limited[1] (HDB). A review of https://helperdb.hk/ shows that HDB is publicly displaying excessive personal data online in contravention of Data Protection Principle 3.


A cursory review of https://helperdb.hk/ shows at least the following types of personal data exposed on publicly accessible web pages and PDFs:


1. Name of Applicant;
2. Address of Applicant;
3. Personal data of the Applicants’ family members on the Internet;
4. Personal data of the Applicants’ former employers on the Internet;
5. Other information such as marital status, religion, horoscope, etc.[2]


We have included screenshots, Archive.org links, and brief explanations for select instances where such personal information is published online.


It is noted that the publishing of voluminous amounts of personal data appears to contradict the guidance given by the Privacy Commissioner For Personal Data in 2014.[3] Given such, it is suggested that the Privacy Commissioner For Personal Data conduct more rigorous inspections, investigations, and enforcement actions against employment agencies who are  operating in contravention to any of the Data Protection Principles.


Sincerely Yours,


Concerned Hong Kong Resident
