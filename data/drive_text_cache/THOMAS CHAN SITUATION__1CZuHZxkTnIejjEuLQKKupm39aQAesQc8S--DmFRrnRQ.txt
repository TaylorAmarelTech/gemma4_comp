Mr. Sylvestre Bello
Secretary of Department of Labour and Employment
Philippine Government


Ref: Concern About Bad Acting Employment Agencies Furnishing False Information To The Philippine Government


Dear Honorable Mr. Bello,


We thank you for your time and consideration of this letter. After listening to the speech made by President Duterte, we believe it is imperative that we share the following information to you - in order to protect OFWs by shedding light on a set of bad acting employment agencies.

Specifically, we have been informed that a group of bad acting agencies are engaged in a corrupt scheme to interfere with the proper regulation of employment agencies. Indeed, it is believed that these agencies have submitted false information to the Philippine Government in order to inhibit the proper regulation of employment agencies.


Based on our observations, the authors of this false information are engaged in illicit agency operations (overcharging, illegal sub-agents, etc) and do not want to be subject to appropriate and just government regulation.


As I am sure you are aware, Republic Act No. 10022 explicitly states it is illegal for an agency to


“[t]o give any false notice, testimony, information or document or commit any act of misrepresentation for the purpose of securing a license or authority under the Labor Code”


Thus this matter must be taken seriously and the agencies involved must promptly be brought to justice. As discussed more below the small group of bad acting employment agencies appear to be engaging in inappropriate communications with the Philippine Government for the sole purpose of maintaining their illicit recruitment activities. As President Duterte has stated, it is necessary for the government to take a strict stance against abusive agencies.

For the purpose of good order, ethical recruitment, and the well-being of OFWs and their families, we wish to submit to you the following observations for your consideration.


Sincerely Yours,

Mirasol C. Ortega and Rubie Bilayre Dionala
Mirasol C. Ortega was witnessed at the Hong Kong Philippines Overseas Labour Office (POLO) and inappropriately went into the private office of one of the Assistant Labour Attaches in Hong Kong. Ms. Ortega proceeded to make misleading accusations against a number of individuals and has spread this questionable information to other bad acting agencies in Hong Kong.


However, brief research on the activities of Ms. Ortega sheds significant light on why she has attempted to interfere with the lawful regulation of agencies. Specifically:


1. Ms. Ortega was the operator of Hong Kong Principle Agency Luckyfull International Limited. Luckyfull International Limited was shutdown for its involvement in multiple illegal recruitment schemes. Indeed, three recruiters of Luckyfull International Limited were arrested in the Philippines for illegal recruitment[1];

2. The partner company of Luckyfull International Limited, JMM Promotion & Management Inc, was also suspended by the POEA. However, Ms. Ortega and her partner, Rubie Bilayre Dionala conspired to create two new agencies in order to continue their illicit agency operations. They continue to overcharge domestic workers with placement fees of at least 45,000 PHP and have even forced workers to buy their own airline tickets for deployment;

3. The new Philippines agency, Medlink International Resourcing Incorporated, is being used by Ms. Dionala to process contracts for these tainted placements and are then sent to Ms. Ortega in Hong Kong via Hong Kong principle agency Grateful Employment Agency. Incredibly, Ms. Ortega and Ms. Dionala having narrowly escaped arrest for their illegal agency actions, continue to operate their new unethical agency that abuse domestic workers;

4. Indeed, Grateful Employment Agency and Medlink International Resourcing Incorporated process contracts for un-accredited sub-agent In Heart Employment Agency Limited. In Heart Employment Agency Limited charges workers an illegal 5,000 HKD placement fee when they arrive in Hong Kong and require workers to obtain illegal loans. It was also observed that In Heart Employment Agency Limited has numerous passports withheld in its office.


In light of the above, it is clear that Ms. Ortega is likely submitting questionable information in order to interfere with POLO’s operations. The actions of Ms. Ortega are likely nothing more than retaliation against POLO and the POEA suspending their ability to recruit workers as a result of their involvement in illegal recruitment activities. Ms. Ortega has also incorrectly stated that workers should not pursue their legal rights because they only come to Hong Kong to work.

Employeasy Group Limited/Employeasy Limited and HelperDB Limited
Finally there exists a substantial conflict of interest between Employeasy Group Limited/Employeasy Limited and HelperDB Limited.


By way of background, Employeasy Group Limited/Employeasy Limited provide and have provided software services to POLO. However, the owners of Employeasy Group Limited/Employeasy Limited have created a new company called HelperDB Limited which is an unaccredited employment agency.

HelperDB Limited, appears to be inappropriately using confidential data from POLO in order to make placements and advertise domestic workers on their website here: https://helperdb.hk


It seems as though Employeasy Group Limited/Employeasy Limited and HelperDB Limited are violating numerous laws including the Hong Kong Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance. Given such the Philippines Government should take prompt action to investigate Employeasy Group Limited/Employeasy Limited and HelperDB Limited to ensure worker privacy and compliance of laws in Hong Kong and the Philippines.
