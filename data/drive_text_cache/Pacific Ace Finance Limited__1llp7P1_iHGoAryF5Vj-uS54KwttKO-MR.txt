Documents relating to the ongoing FamilyHan investigation:         Ms. Jessica Sumpio attempted to submit evidence regarding the illicit behavior of FamilyHan to PCG on or around 6 September 2020. Unfortunately, according to reports, the evidence was not considered. To ensure the concerns of OFWs are considered in full, enclosed are the documents relating to Ms. Sumpio’s complaint.    The documents detail significant illicit activity of FamilyHan, including but not limited to:                 Unlicensed money lending in Hong Kong SAR;                    Irreverent disclosure of personal data;                 Suspected money laundering;                    Excessive interest;                    Harassment;                    Et al.               Authorities in Manila have already instructed OFWs to submit their criminal complaints to PCG, therefore we hope that PCG will intake these complaints and provide a transparent and equal process for OFWs in Hong Kong to report suspected violations of the law. This is very necessary to avoid any further or perceived favoritism.    For example, certain information relating to FamilyHan was accepted by PCG when submitted by another OFW but the information submitted by Ms. Sumpio was reportedly not entertained.    Similarly, PCG appears to have, at least initially, rejected information related to suspected human trafficking and money laundering syndicates involving HOYA Lending Investor Corporation and Prosperity and Success Lending Investor Corporation.                                    Background and importance of taking prompt action:         The egregious behavior of FamilyHan is not only affecting Philippines Nationals. Indeed, the behavior of FamilyHan is negatively impacting thousands of employers in Hong Kong SAR and Singapore. This is occurring primarily in two ways:                   Harassing phone calls, messages, and frequent demand letters with no return address;                  Exposure of personal data points of thousands of employers. In consideration of the above, it is respectfully requested that PCG take all necessary action to protect OFWs from illicit and unlawful lending schemes.    Furthermore, we would like to point out that according to AO 168-13 Manual of Operations, Policies &amp; Guidelines for the POLO &quot;[i]n cases involving criminal and/or police matters and procedures, the POLO shall render assistance at the initial contact with concerns worker but it shall endorse said cases to the Assistance to Nationals (ATN) section of the Philippines Embassy/Consulate.                        Unfortunately, POLO-HK has received numerous agency and money lender complaints concerning issues outside the scope of R.A. No. 8042 and its implementing rules and guidelines. POLO-HK did not take apparent action to forward these concerns to PCG-HK. This lack of action has prejudiced innumerable migrant workers and caused significant damages and delays to their rights to seek legal remedies and be given fair and unbiased access to the criminal complaints systems of the Philippines.    As a concrete example, since at least December 2019, a significant number of OFWs file complaints against CASH4U Financing Corporation and the colluding agency partners. Unfortunately, POLO-HK staff often discouraged OFW victims by falsely stating that CASH4U Financing Corporation had nothing to do with the agency. Even when complaints were submitted, there has been no apparent follow-up to the complaints and there has been no proactive action to investigate CASH4U Financing Corporation or the colluding agency partners.              We ask that a prompt, transparent, and unbiased investigation be conducted in addition to remedial actions. If such evidence uncovers bad actions appropriate administrative action should be taken to avoid these activities from transpiring in the future. Furthermore, victims of illicit lenders and agencies who were discouraged from filing a complaint should be contacted and allowed to submit a full and complete complaint in a courteous and respectful manner.
