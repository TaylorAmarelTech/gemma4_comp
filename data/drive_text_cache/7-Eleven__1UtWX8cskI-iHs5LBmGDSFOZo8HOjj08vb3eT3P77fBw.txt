Binder Progress / Outline:


* Intro: 711 is a multinational corporation with convenience stores and franchises globally. In addition to providing general retail services, 711’s around the world are now providing significant quasi-banking and money transfer services. It has been found that these quasi-baking and money transfer services are generally underregulated and are being used to facilitate (i) Money laundering; (ii) Human Trafficking; and (iii) Other illicit activities.

* Money Laundering
   * Money Laundering in Hong Kong (Organized and serious crimes ordinance S.25) Primary predicate offenses, would be suspicion of excessive interest.
   * CASH4U / Niaga Finance Scheme
   * Summary: 711 is reported to have processed over 10 million HKD in transactions related to money laundering in Hong Kong from DATE to DATE.
   * Legal Argument: The transaction appear to be related to excessive interest loans. Excessive interest is a indictable offense, therefore 711 as an entity that dealt/deals in the proceeds of the suspected or actual indictable offense may be liable for money laundering.
   * Ignorance is not a defence for money laundering
   * Even though 711 didn’t materially profit from the transactions they have still dealt with crime proceeds
   * 711 could have acted faster based on the information they were provided with and had knowledge of, via mail and email.
   * Etc

   * Receipts of each transaction
   * Similarly situated victims and transaction information is provided in Annex X
