WITNESS STATEMENT OF NJENGA ESTHER WANJIKU 


1. I, NJENGA ESTHER WANJIKU, make this statement to report the deceptive trade practices and illicit activities of Talent (Kenya) Company Limited (“Talent Kenya”), which is a Hong Kong limited company and licensed employment agency. 

2. Unless otherwise stated, the facts deposed into this statement are within my personal knowledge, and they are true. Where they are outside my personal knowledge, they are true to the best of my knowledge, information and belief, and are derived from the sources stated in each case. 

3. In June 2018, my aunt’s friend referred me to Khobeshah Agency in Kenya, who had a collaboration with Talent Kenya. I applied and submitted my personal information to Khobeshah Agency and waited for around one year. 

4. In June 2019, I received a text from Ms Ivy Lau of Talent Kenya about a job offer in Hong Kong. She sent the message using the number +852 9729 8341 and asked me if I wanted to join her in Hong Kong. Ivy told me more about Hong Kong as I had never worked in Hong Kong before. She told me that the salary in Hong Kong was better than the Middle East and if I accepted the job offer, I could not get any rest day for the first three months of the employment as it would be my probation period. I did not object to it since I did not know the regulations for migrant domestic workers in Hong Kong. In addition, Ivy told me that I would need to pay Ksh 150,000 to cover the cost of my visa, HKID, medical check, consulate and ticket to Hong Kong. 

5. In June 2019, Ivy arranged an interview with my employer via WhatsApp video call. I could not see my potential employer very clearly in the video call. I did not know the name of my potential employer at the time of the interview. 

6. Later, Ivy told me that she was no longer related to Khobeshah Agency so I should pay Ksh 150,000 (around HKD12,000) to her instead of Khobeshah Agency. In a WhatsApp conversation, she told me to pay Ksh 70,000 as deposit and Ksh 3,000 as document charge to her lawyer called Joan (“Joan”). She sent me Joan’s phone number via WhatsApp, which was +254 727 866602. I contacted Joan using the phone number given and Joan provided me with her address at “Solar House, Harambee Avenue, Opposite Cooperative Bank bob blast, 5th floor”. 

7. On or around 12.06.2019, Ivy also sent me a job offer letter via WhatsApp and asked me to sign the letter. The letter stated that my employer’s name is “Cheung Sze Shan”. I printed out the letter, signed it and returned it to Ivy via WhatsApp. 

8. On 18.06.2019, I went to Joan’s office at the address provided by her and met her female assistant. I did not know the name of Joan’s assistant. Joan’s assistant gave me four sets of blank Standard Employment Contract (“SEC”) to sign. 

9. I saw that clause 8 of the SEC states that my employer has to pay for medical examination fees, visa fees and others, which was different from what Ivy told me. I therefore inquired from Joan, but she said she was just dealing with the papers so I should ask Ivy instead. I took a photo of clauses 7(a) and 8 of the employment contract and sent it to Ivy in a WhatsApp message, asking her what these were. Ivy replied saying that these only apply to Filipino workers.

10. On 18.06.2019, I asked Ivy to confirm once again that I would only have to pay a total of Ksh 150,000 to her and Ksh 3,000 to Joan as document charge, and once I settle this payment, there would not be any more charges. Ivy confirmed and replied saying that this was accurate.

11. I then signed the SEC. After I signed the four sets of SEC, no copy was given to me. On the same day, I asked my father to make the first payment of Ksh 73,000 on my behalf to Joan by bank transfer. Joan confirmed receipt of the payment by giving me a copy of the receipt and asked me to sign on an employment contract. 

12. On 29.07.2019, Ivy confirmed receipt of the Ksh 70,000 that was sent via bank transfer. After she informed me that I had to pay the remaining balance of Ksh 80,000 when my VISA was approved. Later Ivy told me I should collect my visa from a man named Phineus Kiboi (“Phineus”), pay him Ksh 3,000, and transfer the balance of agency fee of Ksh 77,000 through Western Union to a “Chan Yan Wa” in Hong Kong. Ivy had told me that if I failed to pay the said amount, she would cancel my air ticket to Hong Kong. 

13. On 06.08.2019, Ivy told me that I would have to obtain medical reports before departing Kenya as these would be required by Hong Kong Immigration, and that I would be required to pay for it out of my own pocket. I felt aggrieved as Ivy had previously claimed that my payments of Ksh 153,000 to her would cover the cost of this medical examination. However, when I tried to ask Ivy about this, she did not give me a clear answer and said it is a normal process for all domestic workers. When I asked her if I could use my previous medical examination results, Ivy said I could not do so and that there was a requirement for me to take the medical examination one week before I leave my home country. 

14. I accordingly visited Ruaraka Uhai Neema Hospital (“Hospital”) in Nairobi, Kenya for my medical examination. The Hospital issued me two medical reports, a Laboratory Report dated 08.08.2019 and a Chest Radiograph dated 06.08.2019. I had to pay for both these reports out of my own pocket. I have copies of these medical reports with me, but do not have access to the payment receipts issued to me by the Hospital as I did not bring these with me to Hong Kong.

15. On 07.08.2019, I met Phineus and made the payments of Ksh 80,000 in accordance with Ivy’s instructions. I made the payment of Ksh 3,000 to Phineus using M-PESA, a mobile money transfer service in Kenya. I later received a text message alert confirming that this payment had been made. I also made the payment of Ksh 77,000 to “Chan Yan Wa” via Western Union. Ivy confirmed receipt of this payment on 09.08.2019 via WhatsApp.

16. Ivy sent me my flight ticket to Hong Kong on 08.08.2019 and I arrived in Hong Kong on 12.08.2019. 

17. After arriving in Hong Kong, I asked Ivy to clarify my rest day entitlement, since I read from my employment contract that I should get rest days (clause 6). Ivy then admitted that she deliberately told Hong Kong employers that Kenyan workers are willing to not take all of their day off so that employers are more willing to hire more Kenyans. 

18. My employer made me sleep in the open area right beside my employer’s house. That area was intended for the kitchen and laundry room. I had no privacy as there was no door and people passing the area could see inside through the glass shield. There were no curtains or partitions. I was often feeling cold. 

19. In my employment contract, it was stated that I will have a room of 50 square feet. 

20. I did not get any days off for the entire period of my service. 

21. In the end of September 2019, I had a few discussions with my employer about a rest day arrangement. My employer told me that I would be allowed to take my day off on every Friday from 12 noon to 5 p.m. Furthermore, my employer said I would need to work before and after that. I mentioned to my employer that I wanted to go to the church on Sunday. My employer then said she could change one of the Friday off to Sunday, but I would still need to prepare breakfast before going out, and to return home at 5pm. I told my employer that I will think about it. 

22. After a few days, my employer asked for my decision. She told me that either I accept this arrangement, or I write a two-months’ notice to resign from my job. My employer told me that she needs two months’ notice from me as she needs time to find my replacement. Since my employer was not going to grant me whole rest days, I served two months’ notice to her instead. 

23. On 16.10.2019 in the morning, my employer suddenly called my name when I wanted to start cleaning the bathroom. My employer told me to start packing my belongings as she will send me back to the agency. She checked my belongings before we left and told me that she will pay entitlements at the agency. 

24. On the way back to the agency, I told my employer that I will not go to the agency with her because the agency knew my last working day was on 4 December 2019. I then told my employer that I prefer to go to the Labour Department. My employer refused and insisted that I go to the agency with her so that we can terminate the employment contract. I then called an NGO for assistance and my employer made a phone call too. As my employer pulled my luggage and held my bag, I called the police for assistance. When police came, Ivy was with the police and my employer was there too. Police officers came and accompanied me to the MTR station so that I could go to the NGO. 

25. Staff from the NGO explained to me about my rights. I then decided to file claims against my employer at the Labour Department. 

26. My conciliation meeting was scheduled on 30.10.2019 at the Labour Department. No settlement was reached so I had no choice but to transfer my case to the Labour Tribunal. The claim number for this case was LBTC3883/2019 c/w LBTC4017/2019. 

27. On 15 October 2020, the Labour Tribunal ordered the Employer to pay me an amount of HK$23,070.10, which included “Reimbursement of Expenses” in the amount of HK$11,132.64. This amount was partial reimbursement for the Ksh 153,000 (HK$11,584.64) I had to pay to Ivy as outlined in paragraphs 6 - 12 of this Affidavit, above. It did not include HK$452 (around Ksh 6,280) for Talent Kenya’s agency fee. 

28. On 27 October 2020, I found out that Ivy had also lied to me about using the Ksh 153,000 for my flight ticket, as the Employer was the one who had actually paid for it. I was made aware of this when the Employer later adduced evidence to the Labour Tribunal that she had paid HK$4290 for my flight ticket from Nairobi to Hong Kong and HK$1,350 for my insurance. She applied for leave to amend the amount ordered to be paid by her under the 15 October 2020 Award/Order. 

29. On 28 December 2020, the Labour Tribunal granted leave for my employer’s application to review the earlier Award/Order. The Labour Tribunal accordingly amended the Reimbursement of Expenses to HK$5,492.64, reducing the amount by HK$4,290 + HK$1,350 = HK$5,640. The total amount to be paid to me by my employer was accordingly amended to HK$17,430. As such, I was unable to recover the full Ksh 153,000 at the Labour Tribunal.
