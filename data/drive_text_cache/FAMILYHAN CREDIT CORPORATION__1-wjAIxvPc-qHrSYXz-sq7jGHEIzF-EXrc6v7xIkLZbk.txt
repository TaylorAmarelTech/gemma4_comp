REPORT OF ATTEMPTED MONEY LAUNDERING WITH POSTAL SERVICES AND REQUEST FOR ASSISTANCE


To Whom This May Concern,
 
This letter is being sent to report a suspected money laundering scheme involving a Philippines lending company FamilyHan Credit Corporation (“FamilyHan”), its undisclosed Hong Kong partner (the “Hong Kong Partner”), and any persons or entities in Hong Kong seeking to facilitate payments to FamilyHan. It has been observed that the Hong Kong Partner sends demand letters following a particular pattern to the employers of borrowers in an attempt to seek immediate payment from the borrower (also the “victim”). As will be explained below, loans from FamilyHan carry excessive interest rates which would constitute an indictable offence in Hong Kong, hence dealing or attempting to deal with proceeds of the loan monies may be money laundering. Since the Hong Kong Partner is attempting to deal with proceeds of an indictable offence by sending demand letters, it is requested that the Post Office use all tools available to intercept the demand letters for further law enforcement.


Pursuant to the Post Office Ordinance (Cap. 98), the Postmaster General may delay postal packets and its contents if there is reason to believe that they contain anything by means of which any offence is being committed or attempted. “Offence” is defined in the Interpretation and General Clauses Ordinance (Cap. 1) to include any crime and any contravention or other breach of, or failure to comply with, any provision of any law, for which a penalty is provided. The offence of money laundering is clearly within the scope of this definition. It is hence requested that:
1. The Postmaster General exercises such discretion and take immediate measures to delay or refuse delivery of demand letters sent by FamilyHan as they are tools inducing money laundering; 
2. The Post Office and all Postal Services staff assist in tracking the source of these letters; 
3. These measures are strictly enforced until relevant law enforcement in Hong Kong and in the Philippines complete investigations into FamilyHan and the Hong Kong Partner. 




Explanation of Potential Money Laundering
By way of background, FamilyHan is an online money lender based in the Philippines which grants loans carrying excessive interest rates. From the 130 victims we are in contact with, it is believed that most victims in Hong Kong have received at least one demand letter from the Hong Kong Partner, and in some cases, victims have received up to 3 demand letters. 


Borrowers in Hong Kong are asked to repay their loans using remittance companies in Hong Kong to transfer money into FamilyHan’s bank accounts in the Philippines. Indeed, remittance companies have been blocking payments from FamilyHan (see Exhibit 1) suggesting that they too agree with our legal analysis on potential liability for money laundering. On the occasion that remittance companies have failed to take action, crime reports have been made with the relevant authorities in Hong Kong.


From reviewing the contents of a sample of demand letters (Exhibit 2) and a sample loan agreement obtained (Exhibit 3), the effective annual interest rate carried by FamilyHan loans far exceed 180% per annum even without considering alleged “penalty interest”, and interest rates regularly reach 300-400% per annum. Details of such calculations are provided in both Exhibits. Under the Money Lenders Ordinance (Cap. 163), granting a loan with an interest rate exceeding 60% per year constitutes an indictable offense. It follows that the loans are invalid under Hong Kong law and any further collection or dealing with the proceeds of the loan may constitute money laundering under the provisions of the Organized and Serious Crimes Ordinance (Cap. 455). Notably, the money laundering offence may be committed even if FamilyHan’s charging of excessive interest is conducted overseas, as court decisions have authoritatively confirmed that the relevant conduct is assessed under Hong Kong law. A further list of suspected violations by FamilyHan and the Hong Kong Partner is enclosed as Exhibit 4. 


FamilyHan has issued 5378 loans by 23 April 2020, and it is estimated that by 15 June 2020 a total of 7900 loans have been issued, all of which likely carry excessive interest rates. From the sample of victims we are in contact with, the average sum (even without considering extraordinary “late interest”) FamilyHan is seeking to collect per loan is 45,800 PHP. If 7900 such loans have been issued, FamilyHan and the Hong Kong Partner may be engaging in money laundering involving at least HKD 53.8 million.


Identifiers of FamilyHan Demand Letters
Scanned copies of numerous envelopes containing FamilyHan demand letters from March to June 2020 are included as Exhibit 5. An estimated 50 demand letters have been voluntarily provided by victims, many of which remain unopened. These letters may be provided to law enforcement and the Post Office for review and further action.


From initial observations, the following indicators appear to be evident (see Exhibit 5):
1. Envelopes are stamped in red text “Letter of Demand”, usually on the top-left corner;
2. Occasionally the words “Letter of Demand” may be seen in large text as the contents of the letter from the unopened envelope;
3. The first line is the name of a foreign domestic helper followed by a series of letters and numbers in brackets;
   1. The name can be identified as a non-Chinese name, usually with a Spanish or Anglo-American derived first name, middle name and surname; 
   2. A code appearing to be a passport number or other identification number, usually 1 or 2 letters followed by 7 numbers for a total of 9 figures;
   3. The code is in brackets after the full name.
4. Occasionally the words “Your Loan Account No.: XXXX/2020” may be seen as the contents of the letter from the unopened envelope, the “XXXX” representing the loan number of the victim; 
5. Items 2-4 above are in capital letters with a Serif typeface resembling the font Times New Roman; and
6. A single local stamp of $2 HKD is fixed to the envelope.


In consideration of the above, we request that the Post Office use all the tools available to stop FamilyHan from engaging in further illicit activities. The Post Office is strongly urged to refuse or delay delivery of potential demand letters, track down these sources, and alert employees of the scheme. Should it assist investigations and further action, we are happy to provide the Post Office with the original hard copy demand letters received.

	Legal Basis
	Explanation
	Money Lenders Ordinance
(Cap. 163)
	The loans from FamilyHan carry an interest rate exceeding the limit of 60% per annum which is an indictable offence in Hong Kong.
	Organized and Serious Crimes Ordinance (Cap. 455)
	The partner or representative of FamilyHan in Hong Kong and all other entities in Hong Kong seeking to collect or remit the loan monies are dealing or attempting to deal with proceeds of an indictable offence, which may constitute money laundering.
	Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486)
	FamilyHan and its Hong Kong partner are disclosing personal information to third parties without authorization.
	Republic Act No. 3765:
Truth in Lending Act
	FamilyHan operates an online lending platform which fails to provide borrowers with a Disclosure Statement and Loan Agreement as required by law.
	Republic Act No. 10173:
Data Privacy Act
	On multiple occasions, the personal information of borrowers have been disclosed to third parties including a suspected partner or a commonly controlled entity in Hong Kong without authorization. This is a direct violation of the law.
	Republic Act No. 9160: Anti-Money Laundering Act
	The loans are granted through means of estafa (swindling) and therefore constitute unlawful money laundering activity.
	Republic Act No. 9194
	Unlawful activities captured by the Act include “[f]elonies or offenses of a similar nature that are punishable under the penal laws of other countries.” As such, the collection of loan proceeds by FamilyHan constitutes money laundering because the activities of FamilyHan constitute an indictable (felony) offense under Hong Kong’s Money Lenders Ordinance.
	Section 3.01 of the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of Section 4(aa) of the Bayanihan to Heal As One Act
	FamilyHan has attempted to collect loan repayments and continued to demand payment despite the mandatory grace period for all loans and interest payments.

2. Suspected criminal intimidation;
3. Suspected blackmail;

5. Refusing to verify the accuracy of the loan agreements and payments.
