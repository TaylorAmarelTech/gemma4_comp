Dear Facebook Authorities, 


Report of Suspected Illegal Money Lending Activity on Facebook 


We are writing to make a complaint regarding suspected illegal activity facilitated by your platform. It has come to our attention that illegal money lending activity, in particular through an organisation under the name of “OWA”, has been ongoing on Facebook for a period of time. 


Suspected organisations and its operations 


It has been observed that “OWA”, along with many of its affiliates, such as “DH Mutual Aid, GEMS, OFW Finance Company, Get Help HK OFW, Helping Overseas Filipino Workers, Kabayan International Finance Assistance, NEWS Financial Group, Assistance and Protection Micro Finance Corp, True Help Through Life Corporation, Helping Hands Asia Co, Kasangga Corporation, Gabay Pangkaunlaran Finance Corp, Soft Finance International, Advanced Credit”, have been operating an unlicensed money lending business using multiple Facebook pages and/ or accounts which grants excessive interest loans to individuals in Hong Kong. OWA advertises its money-lending business using Facebook posts and communicates with borrowers through Facebook inbox. 


It is suspected that OWA, along with its affiliates:
 
1. Grant loans without a money lenders license in contravention to the Money Lenders Ordinance;
2. Grant loans that carry excessive interest in contravention to the Money Lenders Ordinance;
3. Engage in suspect fraud, blackmail, and other behavior likely constituting criminal intimidation/harassment;
4. Actively violate provisions of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance; and
5. Refuse to provide their physical address or any background information about their lending operations.


Excessive Interest 


Pursuant to the Money Lenders Ordinance, granting a loan with an interest rate exceeding 60% per annum constitutes an indictable offense. The suspects regularly grant loans with interest rates far exceeding the maximum rate of 60% per annum. The loans are usually structured in the following way:


Loan Amount
	2,000 HKD
	Processing Fee
	200 HKD
	Loan Principal
	1,800 HKD
	Total Repayment
	6,000 HKD
	Repayment Schedule
	300 HKD per week for 20 weeks
	Length of Loan
	20 weeks or 0.383561644 of a year
	Interest Charges
	4,200 HKD
	Annual Interest Rate
	606.6684% per year
	

The annual interest rate of 606.6684% per annum, is over 10 times the maximum allowable interest of 60% per year. Prompt action must be taken to remedy this situation.

Other Illicit Behavior:


In addition to the unlicensed lending operation and excessive interest rates being charged - the suspects grant loans to borrowers using misleading, unscrupulous, and fraudulent misrepresentations. Such illicit behavior has been documented in the following forms:


* Threats to borrowers;
* Threats to disclose personal financial information to third parties;
* Threats to post pictures of borrowers on social media;
* Threats to have the police arrest the borrower;


Such activities likely constitute criminal intimidation, violations of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance and other applicable laws. Considering such, it is in the best interest of Facebook to take prompt action to block suspected illicit pages and operations to manage both third-party platform liability and reputational risk.
