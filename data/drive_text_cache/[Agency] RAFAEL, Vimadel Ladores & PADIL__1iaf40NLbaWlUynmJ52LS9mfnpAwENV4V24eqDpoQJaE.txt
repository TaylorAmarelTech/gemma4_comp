In February of 2021, we both responded to Facebook posts authored by Ruby Ortiz Bote advertising job opportunities in Gardening and Domestic Work in Hong Kong. After inquiring about the jobs, Ruby assured us that this was not a scam, but rather a direct hire opportunity for Filipino workers to deploy to Hong Kong by April 2021. We referred several of our relatives to the jobs and Ruby required them to submit passport photos, pictures and resumes. Further, she required them to make a deposit of 50,740 PHP (equivalent to 7,794.33 HKD) for the costs of placement fees, training fees and medical fees and to secure the job. Ruby would not allow us to speak to the other recruiter, but asked us to transfer the proposed amounts to Marilyn Pascua Gregory via Western Union. Four of our family members in the Philippines transferred money to Marilyn via Western Union in February 2021 (please refer to the attached Payment receipts), amounting to a total of 202,960 PHP. BOTE, Ruby Ortiz and GREGORY, Marilyn Pascua purported to offer the following services and accepted payment on account of promising to fulfill the same. The following thus constitutes applying trade description to a service to be supplied: 


1. Claimed that jobs (unskilled) existed in Hong Kong. 
2. Claimed that they would process and provide these job placements 
3. Claimed that they would help to process the job-seekers visas and claimed that they had a personal contact within the Immigration Department, the husband of Ms. Shirley Leung [+852 5238 8901], that was helping to process the applicants’ visas
4. Claimed that they would provide training in the Philippines for the job applicants


These trade descriptions applied to their services, however, were false. The following acts constitute the above mentioned trade descriptions being false.


1. The jobs did not exist. No indications of the availability or further particulars of the job were provided, such as the employer’s name. These facts point to the trade description being false.


2. No documentation was presented to the applicants to indicate the existence of the jobs. 


3. The suspects could not process the visa paperwork for job-seekers as no visa paperwork was completed by the applicants. The applicants could not have been placed or have visa processed without submitting the appropriate paperwork. Nor were any documents submitted by the suspects to the Immigration Department. 
 
All the factors above indicate that the trade description provided directly by the suspects to the victims was false to a material degree or at least misleading, to be taken for a trade description false to a material degree. 


We are reporting in the hopes that the Customs and Excise Department will take action to investigate the suspected illegal behaviour. We are aware that this scam is still ongoing and that there are additional victims currently being recruited by the aforementioned suspects.
