	2. Navigating Google Folders


A. Case Folders


         1. Case folders are usually in one of the following locations:
         1. GMLC Cases folder
         2. Migrasia Investigations folder


         2. Within each case folder, usually the following sub-folders and files can be found:
         1. Client folders (often called “known victims”)
         2. Binder files (if any):
         1. Typology/diagram of the scheme
         2. List of suspects (often called “rap sheets”)
         3. Key figures
         4. Summary of the relevant laws 
         5. Descriptions of violations and supporting evidence
         6. Table of witnesses and their documents
         3. Evidence, including screenshots of conversations, harassment, payment instructions, etc.
         4. Various letters to the lender, agency, authorities, money service operators, banks, etc.
         5. Complaints and reports to authorities 
         6. For lending companies, spreadsheets containing loan information and interest rates


         3. If there are unorganized files which obviously belong into an existing folder, please move the files. 

         4. If there are files such as unorganized letters/scans, please create new folders with the appropriate names.


B. Client Folders
            1. Client folders are usually called “known victims” in each case folder.

            2. Client folders usually contain the following:
               1. Scanned documents, including:
               1. Employment contract
               2. Passport
               3. HKID
               4. Certificates, e.g. training centre, PDOS
               5. Receipts from employment agency, training, medical check, etc.
               6. Employment agency paperwork, e.g. instructions, flyers, business card
               7. Loan paperwork, e.g. payment slips, payment instructions, business card 
               8. Receipts of loan instalments paid
               9. Demand letters received
               2. Screenshots, including:
               1. Conversations with employment agency staff
               2. Conversations with lending company, e.g. instructions for payment, harassment, bank account details
               3. Suspects’ phone numbers, Facebook profiles
               3. Scanned copies of completed letters or forms, including:
               1. POLO and EAA checklist
               2. POLO form
               3. SEC complaint
               4. PDPO data requests
               5. Record of Consumer Council complaint
               6. Record of EAA online complaint
               7. Records of any other letters signed or complaints made
               8. Any letters received by the client sent to our office address
               4. Notes and action plan for client (if applicable)
               5. Support letter
               6. Statements made, e.g.
               1. Police statements
               2. EAA statements


               3. If clients send any of the information above via Messenger, e.g. photo of passport or loan payment slips, immediately upload them to the client’s folder and give the file a suitable name.

 Money Lender Issues 


 Explain the Relevant Laws
                                       9. Explain Hong Kong law: 
Firstly, the loan agreement must be provided, and failure to do so is a violation of the Money Lenders Ordinance. If the loan interest rate exceeds 60%, the loan is illegal under Hong Kong law. (NB. if the loan interest rates are not excessive, e.g. Ceazar/Mutual Honour then skip this) The implications are:
                                          * Any loan carrying excessive interest rate is not enforceable under Hong Kong law;
                                          * Attempts to deal with the loan monies in Hong Kong may constitute dealing with proceeds of an indictable offence;
                                          * If any company in Hong Kong such as a collection company or remittance company accepts payments, they risk committing the offence of money laundering;
                                          * As such, we have been writing to Hong Kong entities involved, asking them to cease and desist from collecting the loan repayments. Our actions have usually resulted in intermediaries (such as banks and 7-Eleven) blocking payments to the money lender. This is likely the reason why some clients will notice it is becoming increasingly difficult to pay in Hong Kong through remittance companies or otherwise.


                                          10. *For HOYA, Cash4U, FamilyHan, or other cases where the loan interest rate is excessive, you may provide an interest rate calculation followed by an explanation of the laws.
                                          * Use Document Studio to generate the loan interest rate calculation. Where this is not possible, calculate this using a simple interest rate calculator and copy the figures into a template (e.g. template for Hoya - do not edit this). This will show the client that their loan is clearly illegal under Hong Kong law.
                                          * For FamilyHan, the interest rate calculation is accompanied by a handout containing the relevant laws.
                                          * For OWA, the standard interest rate calculation is included in the support letter handout.
                                          * For others, you may provide the generic handout on excessive interests.


These documents will be for the client's own reference or supplementary to the support letter for their employer if they wish to show their employer.


                                          11. Explain Philippines law: (Truth in Lending Act), borrowers must be provided with a copy of the loan agreement and a disclosure statement providing certain information including the loan principal and the interest rate. If the client was forced or pressured into taking a loan due to the employment agency, this is also a violation of the law. 

                                          12. Provide a copy of the table of violations for the relevant lending company.


 Initial Advice

If clients are anxious or worried about consequences for not paying, it may be advised that they repay only the actual loan amount received (i.e. the principal) and wait for the information above before paying any alleged interest owed.

6.1. Data Access Request


A PDPO request or data access request is a formal document that can be sent pursuant to the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486). 
                                                   * This is a 7 page form submitted by a Data Subject (our client) to the Data User (company with our client’s personal data, usually an employment agency or lending company). 
                                                   * The Data User upon receiving the PDPO request must respond in writing within 40 days and provide a copy of the information stored regarding the Data Subject, if any.
                                                   * The PDPO request is usually sent to request a copy of loan agreements, receipts, records or other information of a client. It is also sent to apply pressure onto the suspected illegal company, and is often sent with a demand letter. 


 For the majority of ongoing cases there should be a template in the case folder already.


 Completing the PDPO Request
 The form is easy to fill out and only a few entries need to be made. The fields to fill out begin on page 4.


Part I. Data User 
Name - full name of the company ( lender or agency) as shown on corporate documents


Address - full address as shown on corporate documents
	  

	Part II. Data Subject
Name - full name of client, surname first


Personal identifier - client’s HKID no. and/or passport no.


Remaining fields - as shown on the right (or our office address)
	  

	Part III. The Requestor


Irrelevant.


Cross out all fields.
	  

	Part IV. Requested Data
Description - sample on right. Draft the request broadly to include all personal data held including documents signed, agreements, records, etc. and any specific needs for the client

6.2. Letter to Money Lender


A letter is often sent to money lenders to demand that documents such as the loan agreement are provided, and to demand that the money lender cease and desist from harassment, collection attempts, disclosure of information to third parties, etc. 


This guide outlines some of the key components which may be needed. If a letter has already been sent for the particular lender, consider copying it and editing as appropriate. Alternatively consider copying an existing letter from the closest scheme to the one at hand. Some examples of letters previously drafted:
                                                   * Rich Credit
                                                   * Halo/Affinity
                                                   * Niaga (response to letter)
                                                   * OWTEL


                                                   1. Sender, recipient, date
  

	  

	Sender: name of client, our office address or P.O. Box 50499 Sai Ying Pun Post Office
Date: under address of sender
Recipient: full name of company and address as shown on corporate records; email if any; if more than one recipient, create a table (white borders) to add a column. 
	

                                                   2. Heading and Opening
As appropriate - some previous examples include:
  

	  

	  

	  

	

                                                   3. Summary of Problems Identified 
Give a broad account of the illegal/illicit activities of the lender. Draft broadly (and avoid giving too much detail and our cards away) and with appropriate reference to the law (just the Ordinance is enough). If there is insufficient information, skip this entirely. Examples:
I am writing to express my concerns that Rich Credit Finance Limited, its employees, agents, affiliates, directors, managers, shareholders, and controllers (collectively “Rich Credit”), appear to be engaged in a scheme that violates the laws and regulations concerning the operation of money lenders. Indeed, the following illicit activity has been observed:
                                                   1. Charging excessive interest by way of ‘processing’ and ‘savings’ fees, in direct violation of the Money Lenders Ordinance;
                                                   2. Making harassing phone calls to third parties and disclosing personal information without appropriate authorization;
                                                   3. Stealing or otherwise confiscating money the ‘savings’ accounts of clients and advertising ‘savings’ account products despite not having a banking license;
                                                   4. Failing to provide borrowers or references with a copy of various documents, such as loan agreements.
	Reference is made to your 03 December 2019 letter wherein Niaga Finance Company Limited (Niaga) and its director Lie Chau Pin, Paul (Lie), alleged that I am required to make payments amounting 7,500 HKD in three monthly installments due on 2019/09/03, 2019/10/03, and 2019/11/03.

A personal data access request was made to Niaga as well as a demand letter requesting access to data and documents pertaining to the loan. Unfortunately, the responses to these requests were insufficient. After reviewing the enclosed loan agreement (Exhibit 1), it has been determined that the annual interest rate carried by this loan exceeds 60%. Indeed, as shown in Exhibit 1, the loan principal is 35,000 PHP which is equal to 5,386.87 HKD. Furthermore, loan principal was dispersed on or around 2019/07/22.

Furthermore, Exhibit 1 specifically states the borrower "shall pay the total amount payable in 3 monthly installments in Hong Kong Dollar in the amount of TWO THOUSAND FIVE HUNDRED (HKD 2,500) per month starting on 2019/09/03". Such an arrangement provides that I am required to pay 7,500 HKD by 2019/11/03, a timeframe of just 0.29 years which equates to 2113.13 HKD in interest. Using a simple interest rate calculation, the effective interest rate is roughly 135% - and therefore the loan is invalid and any further collection or dealing with the proceeds of the loan would constitute money laundering
	

                                                   4. Cease and Desist
List out all activities the money lender should cease and desist from. Draft broadly and with appropriate reference to law (just the Ordinance is enough). Examples:
In consideration of the above and to prevent Rich Credit from causing further damages, it is demanded that Rich Credit:
                                                   1. Cease and desist from making harassing phone calls and sending messages to myself and any third party. Rich Credit is not authorized to contact my employer or any other person, and Rich Credit is not authorized to send letters to my employer’s address. Attempts to do so will likely violate the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance. This letter constitutes a formal demand for all correspondence hereafter to be addressed to Room 15B1, Cheung Fat Industrial Building, 7-9 Hill Road, Shek Tong Tsui, Hong Kong.
                                                   2. Cease and desist from any collection attempts until I am provided with a copy of the loan agreement or authorization to be a reference as is my right under the Money Lenders Ordinance (extracts as Annex 1);
	I am writing this letter to demand Affinity Finance Limited, Halo World Limited, Shareholders, Staff, Directors, and Affiliated Parties immediately cease and desist from:
                                                   1. Charging excessive interest by way of processing fees, late fees, or other non-interest fees in direct contravention to the Money Lenders Ordinance and SKYLINE CREDIT LTD v LEUNG HING CHUNG - [2019] HKCU 311;
                                                   2. Contacting third parties, harassing third parties, and disclosing personal data and financial information to third parties;
                                                   3. Failing to obtain consent from so called “references” as required by the Licensing Conditions for Money Lenders;
                                                   4. Engaging in all other illicit behavior, including but not limited to money laundering, criminal harassment, criminal intimidation, and other violations of the law.
	

                                                   5. Requests/Demands
List out all demands. Draft broadly and with appropriate reference to law (just the Ordinance is enough). Examples:
In consideration of the above and to prevent Rich Credit from causing further damages, it is demanded that Rich Credit:
                                                   1. Promptly respond to the enclosed Data Access Request made pursuant to the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance and provide a copy of the loan agreement or reference authorization;
                                                   2. Immediately provide a refund of the ‘savings’ accounts to all applicable borrowers or references, ‘savings’ accounts can not be advertised unless Rich Credit is a licensed bank;
                                                   3. Promptly ensure compliance with the Money Lenders Ordinance, Money Lenders Regulations, and Licensing Conditions for Money Lenders.
	Furthermore, it is requested that: 
                                                   1. Pursuant to the requirements under the Money Lenders Ordinance, provide the alleged loan agreement or any other documentation signed.
                                                   2. An explanation is provided as to the relationship between Affinity Finance Limited and Halo World Limited.
                                                   3. The attached data request made pursuant to the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance is responded to.
	In light of the above, it is requested that Niaga promptly provide a letter stating that the loan has been cancelled and is uncollectable for reasons of being non-compliance with the law. Niaga should also take no further action to collect such a loan until proof, if any, can be provided that the loan does not carry excessive interest.

6.3. Consumer Council Complaints 


Consumer Council complaints are intended for consumers to raise concerns so the Council may assist to mediate or negotiate with the trader. For our purposes, we usually use Consumer Council complaints to apply pressure rather than to reach settlement.


                                                      1. Open the Consumer Council website on complaint information.

                                                      2. Generally it is preferable to use the online complaint form.

                                                      3. Provide information on:
                                                         1. The trader, their address, etc.
                                                         2. Brief facts
                                                         3. Desired outcome (usually refund, settlement, and other)
                                                         4. Contact information of client
                                                         5. Supporting documents e.g. receipts, contracts

                                                            4. Download a copy of the complaint and save it to the client folder.

6.4. EAA Complaint


EAA complaints are made against HK employment agencies. The EAA complaint will need to contain specific violations by the employment agency, and will likely require the client to do a formal statement (with an interpreter) at the Labour Department’s office. 


                                                            1. Open the CEAA website on complaint information.

                                                            2. Generally it is preferable to use the online complaint form.


                                                               3. Provide information on:
                                                               1. The agency, their address, etc.
                                                               2. Brief facts
                                                               3. Details of violations
                                                               4. Contact information of client
                                                               5. Supporting documents e.g. receipts, contracts
                                                               6. Client’s day off 
                                                               7. Client’s willingness to give a statement with an interpreter


                                                               4. Download a copy of the complaint and save it to the client folder.

6.5. Police Report


Police reports are made for illegal lenders who engage in criminal activity, e.g. blackmail, criminal intimidation, harassment, operating savings accounts without a license, etc. 


Read background information here: https://www.police.gov.hk/ppp_en/13_faqs/faq_erc.html 


Make a police report here: https://www1.erc.police.gov.hk/cmiserc/EGIS-HK-Web_NEW_UI/ereport_details?report=CRIME_INFMTN&fontSize=100


Keep the details general and avoid putting words into the mouth of clients. Write a short line explaining that the complainant is a domestic worker with Sunday as a day off, and can make a statement with a Tagalog interpreter.

6.6. Customs Report


A report to Customs and Excise is made when there are violations of the Trade Descriptions Ordinance, when money service operators are in breach of rules/regulations/conditions, or other violations are observed and Customs has jurisdiction.


See Customs report details: https://www.customs.gov.hk/en/contact_us/enforcement/index.html


It is easiest to do an email to: crimereport@customs.gov.hk 


Briefly provide information on:
                                                               * The trader, their address, etc.
                                                               * Brief facts
                                                               * Details of violations
                                                               * Contact information of client
                                                               * Supporting documents e.g. receipts, contracts
                                                               * Client’s day off 
                                                               * Client’s willingness to give a statement with an interpreter
