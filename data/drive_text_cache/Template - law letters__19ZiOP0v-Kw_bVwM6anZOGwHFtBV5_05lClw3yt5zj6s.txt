Asean’s activities in this regard constitute serious breaches of Hong Kong laws and regulations, including but not limited to the following:-


1. Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486) (“PDPO”)


* Data Protection Principle (“DPP”) 3 of the PDPO provides that data users are prohibited from using personal information for any purpose which is not or is unrelated to the original purpose for which the information was collected. In the circumstances, information was collected from me in order to facilitate loan repayments. Absent any discernible and lawful justification on why my information has been used in a way that involves my employer, you have breached DPP 3. For the same reason, you may have also breached DPP 1 (which requires personal information to be collected for a lawful purpose directly related to a function or activity of the data user).


* Section 64 of the PDPO prescribes the disclosure of personal information without the data subject’s consent if there is an intent to obtain gain or cause loss to the data subject, or the disclosure causes psychological harm to the data subject. By egregiously disclosing my personal information to my employer, you have put my employment at risk. As a migrant domestic worker, I am in Hong Kong for the purpose of gaining and carrying out employment. Even the risk of an adverse impact to my employment entails significant livelihood consequences to me. Indeed, not only is my livelihood dependent on employment, but my family is also reliant on me. Presently, your unlawful conduct has caused me to suffer significant distress, hurt feelings, and loss of dignity.


2. Money Lenders Ordinance (Cap. 163) 


* Pursuant to the Money Lenders Ordinance (Cap.163) and Skyline Credit Ltd v Leung Hing Chung [2019] HKCFI 169, it is generally illegal to require payment for non-recoverable charges in connection with a loan (such as ‘processing fees’ attached to my loan in this instance). 


ASEAN should immediately cease and desist from all damaging and/or illegal activities and fully comply with the law, including but not limited to the Money Lenders Ordinance, Money Lenders Regulations, Organized and Serious Crimes Ordinance, Crimes Ordinance and Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance.
