Background
Hong Kong is home to more than 200,000 OFWs - unfortunately, the majority of OFWs who come to Hong Kong are subject to illegal, abusive, or unethical fees. In fact, recent surveys and research reports have shown that the average agency fee for OFW coming to Hong Kong is over 45,000 PHP.

The excessive fees charged by employment agencies is very damaging to OFWs and their families. Worse, such fees run contrary to the letter and intent of the laws implemented to protect OFWs. Indeed, the Philippines Government has implemented a policy of "NO PLACEMENT FEE" collection from applicants and deployed Household Service Workers. However, as this letter and other sources will show - OFWs are still be taken advantage of by abusive agencies that often have cosy relationships with the government departments tasked with upholding the rule of law.

In an effort to combat these black market agency activities, numerous OFWs, OFW community groups, and help and support centers have teamed up to not only identify illegal recruitment practices but assist victims of illegal recruitment in exercising their legal rights. This team was formed not for personal gain but for the sole purpose of protecting the next generation of OFWs from abusive agencies.

After looking at over twenty years of industry information, including average fees, frequency of sub-agents, and prevalence of illegal recruitment tactics, it was decided that more needs to be done to combat illicit agencies. Without action, the status quo of abusive employment agencies unjustly profiting off the backs of OFWs would continue and harm the next generation of OFWs. We do not accept the current trajectory of inaction that would preordain the children of the Philippines, our children, to be subject to the same illegal fees and abusive recruitment tactics that we suffered through.

In pursuit of the aforementioned goals, we would like to report the following to your attention:


1. The unfortunate facilitation of improper recruitment schemes by POLO-HK by discouraging agency victims from complaining or threatening complainants that they will be blacklisted if they continue their complaints;

2. A money laundering and illegal recruitment scheme involving at least two money lenders and at least 14 employment agencies. This scheme is estimated to be involved in the laundering of 30,000,000 HKD per year, primarily related to illegal fees, fraud, or excessive interest. by both Mr. Villafuerte and Ms. Obiso;

3. Requests for assistance and recommendations to combat such mal-administration at POLO-HK.

"(6) For a suspended recruitment/manning agency to engage in any kind of recruitment activity including the processing of pending workers' applications; and

"(7) For a recruitment/manning agency or a foreign principal/employer to pass on the overseas Filipino worker or deduct from his or her salary the payment of the cost of insurance fees, premium or other insurance related charges, as provided under the compulsory worker's insurance coverage.

Without prompt action to enforce these provisions to uphold the rule of law, bad acting agencies will continue to collect excessive amounts of money from OFWs and make materially false excuses that they can not be held liable for the actions of medical clinics, training centers, or lending companies. It is well known, or should be well known, within DOLE and the POEA that bad acting agencies will forward OFWs to overcharging medical clinics, training centers, or lending companies and the agencies will receive a portion of the money charged by their partners. In fact, this behavior has been rampant and damaging OFWs for many years and shows that without proper law enforcement the policy of "NO PLACEMENT FEE" can be easily circumvented through these arrangements.

President Duterte and Secretary Bello, if they are sincere in their promises to protect OFWs, should ensure that bad acting agencies are subject to criminal sanctions so their illegal behavior ceases to damage future OFWs. Unfortunately, under the guidance of Secretary Bello, more than 98% of instances of illegal recruitment are referred to Single Entry Approach resolution, rather than a criminal investigation. This has become highly problematic and POEA staff have unfortunately been forced into an unusual situation where the Prosecution Division of the POEA is not given the appropriate resources and directive from superiors to prosecute bad acting employment agencies. On 18 observed occasions in 2019, when victims of illegal recruitment approached the Prosecution Division of the POEA, the POEA refused to intake their criminal complaints and falsely told them they could only pursue an informal civil settlement through Single Entry Approach resolution.

Conclusion

In order to uphold the laws and commitment of the President to root out illegal agencies and recruiters, we ask for your prompt assistance to not only to shutdown abusive agencies but also to hold bad acting civil servants accountable for their involvement in or facilitation of illegal recruitment schemes.


Finally, it is anticipated that POLO-HK will claim that it is working to address these issues. However, evidence does not support these claims. Even when POLO-HK attempts to schedule a reconciliation meeting it does so at an excruciatingly slow pace.

Since Mr. Villafuerte took charge at POLO-HK, the average time for reconciliation has slowed from 2-3 weeks to 2-4 months, meaning many victims of illegal recruitment have to wait an unreasonable amount of time to have a reconciliation meeting. POLO-HK is not providing victims with copies of endorsement letters so if they are terminated or return to the Philippines during the 2-4 month wait, they must restart the entire complaint process at a Philippines POEA. Worse, Mr. Villafuerte and DOLE worked to cancel the improved software service system that would have streamlined the complaint process and improved the reconciliation system for both OFWs and agencies.

In conclusion, the evidence herein shows that POLO-HK is not fulfilling its duties and obligations to protect and welfare of OFWs. In many cases POLO-HK maneuvers in a manner that protects employment agencies, even when the employment agencies have clearly erred.

With continuing deployment of OFWs to Hong Kong, it is necessary that all relevant regulatory bodies use to full force of the law and regulation to hold bad actors accountable - doing anything less will simply perpetuate a broken system where illegal recruiters earn a living off the backs of OFWs.
