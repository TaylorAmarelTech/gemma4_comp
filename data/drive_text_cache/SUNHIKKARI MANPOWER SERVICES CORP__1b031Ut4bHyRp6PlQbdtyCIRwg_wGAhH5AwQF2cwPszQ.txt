4.        Furthermore, Complainant wishes to inform this Forum of the following matters for appropriate due consideration:


      * Complainant, and other similarly situated individuals are pursuing penal (criminal) complaints against Respondent. To avoid impropriety, under no circumstances should a settlement be conditioned on the withdrawal of criminal complaints. As routinely stated by the Philippines Courts, Philippines Administration, and authorities - administration and criminal complaints are allowed to run in parallel and shall largely be treated independently of each other. Furthermore, because the relief available in criminal complaints is entirely different from the relief requested in administrative complaints, there is no forum-shopping provision that would prevent both penal (criminal) and administrative cases from both moving forward;

      * It has been reported that the National Labor Relations Commission (“NLRC”) has asked many victims of Respondent to wait six weeks for a phone call in order to pursue the next step of the NLRC case. I would like to seek clarification on whether or not I can submit additional evidence, motions, or other actions involving the NLRC during this time. I am very worried that during these six weeks, the Respondent may have ex-parte communications with the NLRC and other authorities which would significantly prejudice my rights and my ability to receive compensation through this case. As a result, I respectfully request the NLRC provide clarification on why there is a six week wait, how I can submit communications/evidence, and under what conditions are ex-parte communications allowed? If there are detailed NLRC rules of procedure I would be most grateful to receive a copy;

      * There at at least 15 victims of Respondent with substantially similar cases. To my knowledge 4 of these victims have filed at the NLRC, however, at least another 11 are considering filing and it may make sense for future filings to be consolidated into this case or other complaints against Respondent;

      * It has been observed that Mr. Hernando Reyes, an apparent lawyer for Respondent, previously worked at the Philippines Overseas Employment Administration (“POEA”). Mr. Reyes also appears to have threatened numerous victims that they will end up “forum shopping” if they proceed with penal (criminal) and administrative cases simultaneously. Mr. Reyes, as a former POEA employee, undoubtedly would know that penal (criminal) and administrative cases are separate. With the information at hand it manifests that Mr. Reyes has and may still be leveraging his status as a former POEA employee in order to inappropriately intimidate the victims in an attempt to protect Respondent and potentially Mr. Reyes himself.
