URGENT - REPORT OF SUSPECTED INTERNATIONAL MONEY LAUNDERING SYNDICATE


To Whom This May Concern,


This letter is being sent to report a suspected money laundering scheme involving Hong Kong incorporated company Owtel Limited (“Owtel”), affiliated companies (inside and outside of Hong Kong), and individual directors and ultimate beneficial owners.

Particularly, the three enclosed dossiers detail significant concerns of money laundering and indictable offences reportedly being committed by your client, Owtel, and involving Owtel’s China Construction Bank Account.


As manifested in the enclosed dossiers, Owtel is documented to be involved in an international syndicate that preys on minorities in Hong Kong SAR, Taiwan, Singapore, Macau, and other countries or jurisdictions. Among a copious amount of concerns, are the highly documented activities of excessive interest lending and criminal intimidation in Hong Kong SAR.


Both excessive interest lending and criminal intimidation are indictable offenses, therefore we feel it is of utmost importance to warn China Construction Bank that providing banking services to Owtel may result in China Construction Bank indirectly or directly dealing in funds derived from indictable offenses.


Furthermore, it is worth emphasizing that:


1. Owtel has strong connections to the Philippines and much of the business operations and financial transactions of Owtel involve activities connected to the Philippines. However, the Philippines was recently greylisted by FATF - warranting immediate execution of increased due diligence and monitoring of Owtel’s activities.

2. It has been reported that the China Construction Bank account held by Owtel is being used as a front for an Owtel affiliated company, namely, Ace Power Limited. This suspicion arises from the fact that the accounts held by Ace Power Limited appear to have been frozen by AML/CFT teams or law enforcement authorities and now Owtel is redirecting payments from an Ace Power Limited account to a China Construction Bank account held by Owtel.


It is demanded that China Construction Bank immediately take all measures available to prevent the flow of illicit money within the financial system, especially given there is suspicion that the apparent masterminds of the scheme reside in the Philippines and it is reasonably expected that the funds will be transferred out of Hong Kong. It is noted that China Construction Bank has legal obligations to comply with various local and global AML/CFT laws, regulations, and standards.


To reiterate, a significant amount of funds, if not all funds, being funneled through the China Construction Bank account held by Owtel are suspected to be proceeds of at least the following indictable offenses:

   * Excessive commission pursuant to the Money Lenders Ordinance: Specifically, Owtel grants loans in excess of 60%, and on numerous occasions reported to exceed 100%. According to both the Money Lenders Ordinance and relevant case law, the undisclosed “processing” and “application” fees charged by Owtel can be considered interest fees.




   * Fraud and intimidation: Owtel, in coordination with other entities or its other agents and representatives are engaged in a scheme to defraud, blackmail, harass, and intimidate borrowers by disclosing their personal information to third parties without authorization, threatening alleged borrowers with six years in jail, and threatening borrowers with immigration blacklisting, being placed on a no fly list, and being subject to sham cases. Such misuse and deceptive collection tactics likely constitutes fraud, criminal intimidation, theft by deception, as well as ubiquitous violations of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance.


In addition to the exhibits overleaf, please see the attached dossiers containing extensive evidence and analysis of the money laundering scheme as documented across several months. It is understood that numerous authorities and regulators in Hong Kong SAR, Singapore, and the Philippines are already in the course of investigations into Owtel’s illicit conduct.
