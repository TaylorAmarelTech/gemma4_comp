        Kim Carla De Villa Sarmiento (“Complainant”) submits this filing for consideration before this Forum in relation to the above mentioned case.


1. Complainant requests that the Philippines Overseas Employment Administration (“POEA”) provide a referral letter to the National Labor Relation Commission (“NLRC”). Complainant makes this request for a referral to the NLRC in order to pursue civil claim(s) against the Respondent. In accordance with - G.R. No. 200811 - JULITA M. ALDOVINO, JOAN B. LAGRIMAS, WINNIE B. LINGAT, CHITA A. SALES, SHERLY L. GUINTO, REVILLA S. DE JESUS, AND LAILA V. ORPILLA, PETITIONERS, v. GOLD AND GREEN MANPOWER MANAGEMENT AND DEVELOPMENT SERVICES, INC., SAGE INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT COMPANY, LTD., AND ALBERTO C. ALVINA, RESPONDENTS (“G.R. No. 200811”) - Complainant has the irrevocable right to pursue civil claims in order to recover the full amount owed to them. G.R. No. 200811 specifically states: “Quitclaims do not bar employees from filing labor complaints and demanding benefits to which they are legally entitled. They are "ineffective in barring recovery of the full measure of a worker's rights, and the acceptance of benefits therefrom does not amount to estoppel."The law does not recognize agreements that result in compensation less than what is mandated by law. These quitclaims do not prevent employees from subsequently claiming benefits to which they are legally entitled.” Therefore, Claimant has the inviolable right to pursue a case at the NLRC, regardless of any alleged settlement or quitclaim. In recognition of this, Complainant requests the POEA issue a referral letter to the NLRC.

2. Furthermore, Complainant also affirms that it is Complainant’s desire and intention to provide evidence and testimony to this forum, the POEA, regardless of the result of any real or alleged civil settlement, indeed it is in the interest of the public and justice to ensure law enforcement and administrative bodies receive all evidence in the performance of their regulatory functions. Thus Complainant requests that the administrative case against Respondent continue and Complainant is willing to provide written or in person testimony.
