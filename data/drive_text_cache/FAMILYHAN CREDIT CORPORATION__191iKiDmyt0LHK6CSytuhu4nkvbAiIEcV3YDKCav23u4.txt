URGENT - REPORT OF SUSPECTED INTERNATIONAL MONEY LAUNDERING SCHEME


To Whom This May Concern,


This letter is being sent to report a suspected money laundering scheme involving a Hong Kong incorporated company Own Man (Holdings) Limited (“Own Man”), a Philippines incorporated company FamilyHan Credit Corporation (“FamilyHan”), and at least one bank account of Own Man with CMB Wing Lung Bank, namely with account number 601-343-0208-8 (the “Bank Account”).


By way of background, FamilyHan is an online money lender based in the Philippines which grants loans carrying excessive interest rates and is suspected of using threats and intimidations to incite payment. Victims of the scheme are instructed by demand letters bearing the contact information of FamilyHan to repay the said loans carrying excessive interest rates into the Bank Account (see Exhibit 1). Furthermore, as seen in Exhibit 2, remittance companies are already blocking payments to FamilyHan’s bank accounts in the Philippines, suggesting they agree with our analysis below and agree that urgent action is justified. It is likely that the use of Own Man and the Bank Account is an apparent attempt to bypass the urgent actions already taken by remittance companies to prevent liability for money laundering. 


It is demanded that CMB Wing Lung Bank immediately take all measures available to prevent the flow of illicit money within the financial system, especially given there is suspicion that the apparent masterminds of the scheme reside in the Philippines and it is reasonably expected that the funds will be transferred out of Hong Kong. It is noted that CMB Wing Lung Bank has legal obligations to comply with various local and global AML/CFT laws, regulations, and standards.


The funds within, being sent from, and received by the Bank Account are suspected to be proceeds of at least the following indictable offenses:

* Excessive commission pursuant to the Money Lenders Ordinance: Specifically, FamilyHan grants loans in excess of 60%, and on numerous occasions reported to exceed 300%. According to both the Money Lenders Ordinance and relevant case law, the undisclosed “processing” and “application” fees charged by FamilyHan can be considered interest fees.
________________




* Fraud and intimidation: FamilyHan, in coordination with Own Man or its other agents and representatives are engaged in a scheme to defraud, blackmail, harass, and intimidate borrowers by disclosing their personal information to third parties without authorization. Such misuse and deceptive collection of personal information likely constitutes fraud as well as ubiquitous violations of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance.


In addition to the exhibits overleaf, please see the attached dossier containing extensive evidence and analysis of the money laundering scheme as documented across several months. It is understood that numerous authorities and regulators in the Philippines are already in the course of investigations into FamilyHan’s illicit conduct.
